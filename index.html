Course introduction

What the course is about

The course covers the foundations and applications of Natural Language Processing, from text processing and linguistic levels to parsing, semantics, information extraction, summarization, question answering, chatbots, machine translation, and statistical/neural methods for core NLP tasks.

Skills and knowledge focus

  • Text preprocessing, morphology, POS tagging, n‑grams, language modeling, vectorization, and word embeddings.
  • Syntax parsing (constituency and dependency), grammar formalisms, ambiguity resolution, and error detection with parse trees and dependency graphs.
  • Semantics and disambiguation: word sense disambiguation, lexical/structural ambiguity, semantic parsing, and representation.
  • Information extraction with tokenization, POS, dependency graphs, NER; document summarization (extractive/abstractive), QA, chatbots (retrieval/generative), NLU/NLG, and MT.
  • Statistical and neural approaches across tasks: HMM/CRF for tagging/NER, TF‑IDF/BM25 for IR, Naive Bayes/SVM for classification, LDA for topics, sequence labeling, and transformer-based methods.

What was expected to learn

Build working intuition for linguistic levels, implement parsing and semantic techniques, design IE/NER pipelines, create summaries/QA/chatbots/MT components, and apply statistical and neural models with appropriate evaluation.

Learning objectives

Unit 1 — Introduction to NLP

  • Explain NLP scope, applications, and the need to process unstructured text with steps spanning morphology, syntax, semantics, and pragmatics.
  • Apply tokenization, stemming, lemmatization, POS tagging, n‑grams, collocations, and language modeling to prepare and analyze text.
  • Compare rule‑based, statistical, and ML approaches and select libraries like NLTK, spaCy, Gensim for tasks.

Unit 2 — Syntax & Semantics

  • Build and interpret constituency/dependency parses, analyze grammatical relations, and resolve parsing ambiguity.
  • Perform semantic analysis: lexical semantics, synonymy/antonymy, polysemy, homonymy, hyponymy, and semantic parsing to logical forms.
  • Execute word sense disambiguation and pronoun resolution strategies with knowledge‑based and statistical techniques.

Unit 3 — Semantic relations & discourse

  • Model intent classification with classic ML and transformer embeddings; design training/validation for intents.
  • Extract and generate paraphrases using rule‑based and transformer methods, and evaluate intrinsically/extrinsically.
  • Analyze discourse structure, coherence, coreference, and perform mention detection and clustering with modern models.

Unit 4 — Information extraction & applications

  • Implement IE pipelines: tokenization, POS, dependency graphs, and NER for entities/relations across domains.
  • Build summarization (extractive vs. abstractive), multi‑document fusion, QA systems, and semantic search.
  • Design chatbots (retrieval/generative), NLU/NLG flows, and study classical to neural machine translation types.

Unit 5 — Statistical approaches

  • Apply statistical models for POS/NER (HMM/CRF), language modeling (n‑grams/HMM), and sequence labeling.
  • Use TF‑IDF/BM25 for IR, Naive Bayes/SVM for classification, LDA for topics, and evaluate summarization/sentiment models.
  • Compare classical ML vs. neural methods, address feature engineering and dimensionality challenges, and use similarity metrics (cosine/Jaccard) appropriately.

ResuMate – NLP-Based AI Resume Evaluator

Live/Repo:

Project description

ResuMate is a smart ATS-style evaluator combining NLP and ML to analyze resumes, compare them with job descriptions, and surface skill gaps, grammar issues, and content weaknesses. It includes an interactive AI Chatbot Assistant powered by LangChain + Groq for personalized improvements, all in a streamlined Streamlit web interface.

Key features

  • Resume upload & parsing for PDF/DOCX with robust preprocessing .
  • JD matching via TF‑IDF vectors and cosine similarity scoring for alignment .
  • Grammar and readability checks using language_tool_python and textstat for clarity .
  • AI Chatbot Assistant with LangChain + Groq LLM for tailored feedback and rewrites .
  • Composite scoring that blends similarity, grammar, and readability into a Resume Strength Score .
  • Leaderboard to rank resumes against peers or iterations over time .
  • Caching of NLP models for fast, repeatable evaluations in production runs .

Tech stack

  • Frontend/UI: Streamlit + custom CSS for an intuitive evaluator UI .
  • NLP/ML: spaCy, scikit‑learn (TF‑IDF, cosine similarity), TextStat, LanguageTool for quality metrics .
  • AI Integration: LangChain orchestration with Groq LLM for interactive suggestions .
  • Database: SQLite3 for scores, runs, and leaderboard persistence .
  • Utilities: PyPDF2, python‑docx, Streamlit caching for I/O and performance .

Evaluation process

  1. Extract text from uploaded resume (PDF/DOCX) .
  2. Clean, tokenize, and vectorize with spaCy + TF‑IDF .
  3. Compute JD similarity via cosine similarity .
  4. Analyze grammar with LanguageTool and readability with TextStat .
  5. Aggregate weighted metrics into a Final Resume Score; store and update leaderboard .
  6. Enable AI Chatbot to propose measurable, concise improvements aligned to the JD .

Sample output

Grammar Accuracy92%
Readability Index70%
Overall Score83.5 / 100
"Your resume is strong in skills but could include measurable results in your projects. Try rephrasing statements like 'Worked on NLP models' to 'Improved text classification accuracy by 15%.'"

Future enhancements

  • RAG‑based evaluation with vector DBs (ChromaDB) for semantic gap analysis .
  • Multi‑resume comparison and version tracking for iterative improvement .
  • Automated PDF feedback reports with inline suggestions and scores .
  • Domain‑specific optimization prompts and scoring rubrics via specialized LLMs .

Case Study: Intent Detection and Classification in NLP

Overview

This case study explores how machines interpret user inputs and map them to intent classes (e.g., BookRestaurant, PlayMusic, GetWeatherForecast), with emphasis on chatbot and virtual assistant scenarios .

Objectives

  • Understand end‑to‑end intent pipelines from data prep to deployment in real apps .
  • Compare rule‑based, classic ML (Logistic Regression, Decision Trees), and deep learning (LSTM, BERT) approaches .
  • Prototype an intent recognizer inside Octa Planner to trigger real actions from text .

Methodology

  1. Concept framing: Defined intent schemas and representative classes aligned to assistant commands .
  2. Data preparation: Collected queries, cleaned/tokenized text, and converted inputs to embeddings or TF‑IDF as needed .
  3. Modeling: Built baselines with rule patterns and ML classifiers, then fine‑tuned Transformer embeddings (BERT) for context‑aware classification .
  4. Validation: Split train/validation, monitored precision/recall/F1, and tested generalization on paraphrased queries .

Demo implementation (Octa Planner)

A live prototype parses user text and triggers planner actions; e.g., "Add meeting with team tomorrow at 10 AM" → intent ScheduleEvent with slot extraction for date/time, which auto‑creates the calendar entry in the UI .

Industrial grounding

Inspired by Samsung PRISM themes—contextual understanding and multi‑intent handling—the design adapts these ideas to a compact academic prototype, emphasizing robustness to varied phrasing and context carry‑over .

Key takeaways

  • Transformers (BERT) improved robustness to paraphrase and noisy inputs versus rule/TF‑IDF baselines .
  • Clean labeling and diverse examples remain critical to minimize intent confusion in production .
  • Tight integration with app actions (Octa Planner) shows tangible UX gains from accurate intent mapping .

What's next

  • Extend to multi‑intent and slot filling with joint models for end‑to‑end NLU .
  • Add paraphrase data augmentation and domain adaptation for new command families .
  • Instrument evaluation with confusion analysis dashboards for continuous improvement .
